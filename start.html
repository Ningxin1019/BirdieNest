Nest-Grafting
Manifesto

On the ruins, nesting is instinct
Displacement is destiny
We pick up plastic films
broken phrases, rusted concepts
forgotten formats, from the gaps of time
that do not belong to us
We tuck them into our tail feathers
as a clumsy yet sincere ornamentation
We no longer possess complete materials
No one gifts us clean twigs or soft moss anymore
What remains are drifting waste words
wrinkled interfaces
the remnants of yesterday's templates
But the nest must grow
Even if suspended at the edge of speed
spinning alone in the wrong context
We graft
we displace
we remix
we appropriate
We refuse empty purity
and reject withered order
Let heterogeneity stitch itself here
Let fragility become structure
Let the temporary become eternal
We are a flock
drifting through the ruins of the internet
the crevices of cities
the relics of language
With the wrong materials, we build the right home
—— Nest-Grafting
